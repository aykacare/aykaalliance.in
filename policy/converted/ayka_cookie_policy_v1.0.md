# 2. WHAT ARE COOKIES AND SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES?
## 2.1 Cookies
Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to website operators about how their site is being used. Cookies are not programs and cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device.
## 2.2 Similar Technologies
In addition to cookies, we may use the following similar tracking technologies on our Platforms:
- Web Beacons (Pixel Tags): Tiny invisible images embedded in web pages or emails that allow us to track whether a page has been viewed or an email has been opened. We use web beacons in connection with the Meta Pixel and Google Ads tracking.
- Local Storage: A browser-based mechanism that allows websites to store data locally on your device, used for session management and user preferences that persist between visits.
- Session Storage: Similar to local storage but data is cleared when the browser session ends; used for temporary in-session data such as consultation booking progress.
- Fingerprinting: We do not use browser fingerprinting or device fingerprinting for tracking purposes.
## 2.3 First-Party vs Third-Party Cookies
Cookies may be set by us ("first-party cookies") or by third-party services operating on our Platforms ("third-party cookies"). First-party cookies are set by AYKA Clyvora Private Limited and are used to operate and improve our Platforms. Third-party cookies are set by our service providers — including Meta, Google, and our payment gateway — and are governed by their respective privacy and cookie policies.

# 3. CATEGORIES OF COOKIES WE USE
We use four categories of cookies on our Platforms. The category determines whether your consent is required and how you can manage the cookie.

## 3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies
*These cookies are essential for the Platform to function and cannot be disabled. No consent is required for strictly necessary cookies.*

Strictly necessary cookies enable core functionality such as:
- User authentication and secure login session management;
- Maintaining your session as you navigate between pages;
- Security features including CSRF protection and fraud prevention;
- Load balancing to ensure Platform stability;
- Remembering items in your consultation booking cart or appointment queue; and
- Ensuring compliance with applicable legal and security requirements.
Without these cookies, our Platforms cannot function properly and core services cannot be provided. You cannot opt out of strictly necessary cookies through our consent manager; however, you may disable them through your browser settings, which will significantly impair your ability to use our Platforms.

## 3.2 Functional / Preference Cookies
*These cookies enhance your experience by remembering choices you make. They require your consent.*

Functional cookies allow us to remember information that changes the way our Platforms behave or look, including:
- Your language and regional preferences;
- Your login credentials (if you select "Remember Me");
- Your preferred consultation type or doctor specialty;
- Display preferences such as font size or interface layout; and
- Notifications you have dismissed or actions you have already completed.
If you disable functional cookies, some features of the Platform may not work as expected and you may need to re-enter preferences each time you visit.

## 3.3 Analytics and Performance Cookies
*These cookies help us understand how visitors use our Platforms. They collect anonymised data and require your consent.*

Analytics cookies collect information about how users interact with our Platforms, including:
- Which pages are visited most frequently;
- How long users spend on each page;
- Where users come from (referral source);
- Which features are most used; and
- Error messages encountered by users.
All analytics data is aggregated and anonymised where possible. We use Google Analytics 4 for this purpose. The data helps us improve the performance, usability, and content of our Platforms. We have enabled IP anonymisation on Google Analytics and have executed a Data Processing Agreement with Google.

## 3.4 Marketing and Targeting Cookies
*These cookies track your activity across websites to deliver targeted advertising. They require your explicit consent and can be declined without affecting Platform functionality.*

Marketing cookies are used to:
- Measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns on Meta (Facebook and Instagram) and Google Ads;
- Deliver relevant advertisements to you on Meta and Google platforms based on your interactions with our Platforms;
- Build custom audiences for retargeting campaigns (e.g., users who visited the doctor registration page);
- Prevent the same advertisement from being shown to you too frequently (frequency capping); and
- Track conversions — i.e., whether you completed a registration or booking after clicking an advertisement.
These cookies are set by Meta (via Meta Pixel) and Google (via Google Ads conversion tracking and Google Tag Manager). Their use is governed by Meta's and Google's respective privacy policies and cookie policies, in addition to this Policy.

# 6. MANAGING YOUR COOKIE PREFERENCES
## 6.1 Cookie Consent Banner
When you first visit any of our Platforms, a cookie consent banner will be displayed. You may accept all cookies, accept only strictly necessary cookies, or customise your preferences by category. Your choices will be stored in the consent_status cookie for twelve (12) months.
You may update your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Preferences" or "Manage Cookies" link in the footer of any of our websites. This will re-open the consent manager and allow you to change your selections.
## 6.2 Browser Settings
All modern web browsers allow you to control cookies through browser settings. You can typically:
- View and delete individual cookies stored on your device;
- Block all cookies (note: this will significantly impair Platform functionality);
- Block third-party cookies while allowing first-party cookies;
- Set your browser to alert you when a cookie is being placed; and
- Enable "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals (see Section 7).
Browser-specific instructions for managing cookies:
**Google Chrome: **https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647
**Mozilla Firefox: **https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/enable-and-disable-cookies-website-preferences
**Apple Safari: **https://support.apple.com/guide/safari/manage-cookies-sfri11471/mac
**Microsoft Edge: **https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-edge/delete-cookies-in-microsoft-edge-63947406-40ac-c3b8-57b9-2a946a29ae09
## 6.3 Opting Out of Specific Third-Party Tracking
You may opt out of specific third-party tracking through the following mechanisms, independent of our cookie consent banner:
- Meta Ad Tracking: Visit https://www.facebook.com/ads/preferences/ and manage your ad settings. You can also opt out of interest-based advertising through the Digital Advertising Alliance at https://youradchoices.com/
- Google Analytics: Install the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on available at https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout
- Google Ads Personalisation: Visit https://adssettings.google.com/ to manage or opt out of personalised Google Ads.
## 6.4 Mobile Devices
On mobile devices, you may limit ad tracking through your device settings:
- iOS (Apple): Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → toggle off "Allow Apps to Request to Track"
- Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads → "Opt out of Ads Personalisation"

# 7. DO NOT TRACK (DNT) SIGNALS
Some web browsers transmit "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals to websites. Currently, there is no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. Our Platforms do not alter their data collection and use practices in response to DNT signals from browsers.
However, you may use the methods described in Section 6 to manage your cookie preferences and opt out of specific tracking technologies. We will continue to monitor developments in the DNT standard and update our practices accordingly.
